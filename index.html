1. Background & Business Objective

Why this analysis matters

Proving the Business Value of Converting Normal Customers to Members

Initial exploratory data analysis (EDA) revealed a significant gap in transaction value between "Normal" customers and loyalty "Members".

The objective of this analysis is to identify where this spending variance originates (quantity vs. choice of product vs. geographical location) and deliver actionable store recommendations to convert general foot traffic into high-value members effectively.

5. Data-Driven Recommendations

Actionable Recommendations

Two-Pronged Approach to Drive Exponential Membership Growth

1. Region-Specific Floor Plan & Layout Optimization

Chicago Store: Relocate the "Fruits & Fresh Produce" section directly to the primary entrance zone (A-Rank Space). Capitalizing on visual high-volume items drives customer curiosity and provides an immediate touchpoint for membership sign-up pitches.

Los Angeles Store: Position high-margin "Personal Care" items directly along the main foot traffic path leading toward the high-demand "Beverages" section to induce incremental cross-selling.

2. Targeted Loyalty Perks for High-Frequency Categories

LA: Beverage Incentives

Launch member-only bulk discounts on cases and exclusive early access to premium beverage releases.

Chicago: Produce Perks

Introduce exclusive member morning markets and a 3x pointmultiplier on fresh local produce.

Chain-Wide: Bundling

Deploy "Cross-Category Bundles" (e.g., Personal Care + Fruits) with an exclusive 5% member discount.
